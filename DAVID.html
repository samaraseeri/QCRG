Prof. David Keyes

Bio

Prof. David Keyes is recognized as one of the 35 legends in high-performance computing and serves as a Professor of Applied Mathematics, Computer Science, and Mechanical Engineering at KAUST (King Abdullah University of Science and Technology). He will be the keynote speaker at the Symposium of Quantum Computing & Technology at King Fahd University of Petroleum & Minerals - KFUPM and at the LEAP in Riyadh, discussing "Shaping Economics of Future Technologies and People."

As a founding Dean and Director of the Extreme Computing Research Center since 2009, Dr. Keyes has focused on scalable solvers for power-aware architectures. His collaborations in energy and environmental applications have led to groundbreaking methods, including:

  • Newton-Krylov-Schwarz (NKS)
  • Pseudo-Transient Continuation (ÏˆTC)
  • Additive Schwarz Preconditioned Inexact Newton (ASPIN)
  • Hierarchical Computations on Manycore Architectures (HiCMA)

Before joining KAUST, he led projects at the U.S. Department of Energy (DoE) and contributed to NASA collaborations while teaching at Columbia, Old Dominion, and Yale. A Fellow of SIAM, AMS, and AAAS, he has received numerous awards, including two Gordon Bell Prize, the last in climate in 2024, and HPCWire recognition as one of the 35 "legends" in high-performance computing.
