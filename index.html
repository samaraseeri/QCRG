Welcome to the KAUST Quantum Computing Reading Group

About Us

This webpage serves as a central hub for the KAUST Quantum Computing Reading Group (QCRG). Led by Professor David Keyes and Dr. Samar Aseeri, the group aims to explore the world of quantum computing and its implications for science and technology.

Short-Term Goals

  • Identify research areas at KAUST that can benefit from quantum computation
  • Prepare for a significant thrust in the Economies of the Future RDIA pillar
  • Propose a list of speakers for workshops on quantum computation
  • Get ready to utilize quantum hardware from NEOM and Aramco
  • Evaluate the evolving implications of quantum computing

Long-Term Prospects

  • Establish specialty groups for in-depth exploration of quantum computation
  • Develop a syllabus for a graduate course in quantum computation

Logistics

The group meets every other week for an hour, with the option to join via video conference.

Besides quantum computation, Quantum Information Sciences (QIS) also include quantum sensing and quantum communication, which have their own communities at KAUST.

In October 2023, commercial quantum computing companies conducted orientation sessions at KAUST, with more companies showing interest in developing the quantum computing ecosystem.

KAUST's Shaheen-3 platform will emulate up to 50 reliable qbits for quantum algorithm development.

Saudi Background

Quantum technologies, including quantum computing, are part of Saudi Arabia's Research, Development and Innovation Authority (RDIA) "Economies of the Future" Pillar.

Saudi Arabia is a member of the World Economic Forum's Quantum Economy Hub, aligning with the goals of Vision 2030 to diversify the economy and embrace quantum technologies for various sectors.
